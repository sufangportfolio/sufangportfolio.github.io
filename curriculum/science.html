To incorporate STEAM into teaching does not mean classroom teachers should teach these subjects as stand-alone subjects. To me, STEAM embodies a series of essential skills that are necessary for students to become 21st century ready learners. These skills include critical thinking, problem solving, mathematically thinking and hands-on skill. Through exploring materials, tools and processes, students also learn about grit and resilience.

In my Investigation sessions, my room is transferred into a mini Maker Space. Students get to experience the design cycle, reflecting on their design, prototypes and improvement.

  • Specific teaching of how to improve our works. Think, Make, Improve
  • Improve includes how to make it more stable/prettier/lighter/useful/greener/cheaper/interesting
  • Prettier – use art; more stable – engineering and maths; more useful – tech
  • Use hands on activities to teach concepts e.g. pancake making, foam color
