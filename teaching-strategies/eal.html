EAL

For those who have just arrived with no English

  • For parents: provide welcome package that introduces how the school works, e.g. value, behavioral expectation, uniform requirement; how the class works, e.g. classroom rules, timetable. Ask the family how everything is going. Example: Dunsin’s family.
  • For student: first things to learn, may I have, may I go. Quick test of English skill. Keep everything visual. Pair the student up with a buddy. Learn how to greet in that language. Allow the kid to remain silent. Play games that the kid can jump in right away e.g. craft, dress up. Read literature/music they are familiar with, e.g. Dunsin to build connections. Talk to parents about how to help kids at home. Clear and visual routine. Special Toolbox with activities they are familiar with.

For those who have been in Australia for some time and have English skills

  • Use EAL beginner booklet and language tool box
  • Create a lot of language experience, e.g. take a walk, teach-OK, play a game (barrier game, info sharing, ranking), hands on activity (slime making, cooking), circle time, think pair share, reader’s theatre, investigation.
  • Use music/chants/puppets
  • More waiting time, slow pace, using gesture, check for understanding, leveled questions
  • Sentence frame/4 squares
  • Using images (to generate writing e.g. writing prompts on the writing table, to provide language support e.g. learning about community, to connect to concepts e.g. venn diagram of literature comparison)
  • Modeled and Shared writing and talking
  • Phonics (Oz phonics, words their way)
  • Specific teaching of vocabulary and grammar, also in math and investigation lessons
