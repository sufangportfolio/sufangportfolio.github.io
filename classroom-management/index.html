Classroom culture

  • Morning message/letter
  • Create a Behavioral Management Plan and teach it to students through modeling and practice
  • Clear routine and verbal/non-verbal signals
  • Create a culture that values Growth Mindset and risk taking
  • Share my story with students to build connections
  • Use behavior reward chart
  • Use programs to teach social/emotional skills e.g. Bounce Back

Student voice

Student Voice describes how students give their input to what happens within the school and classroom.

  • Students initiate topics to explore in investigation
  • Goal setting and badge system
  • Expert profile
  • Self and peer assessment
  • TIB for learning intentions
  • Provide choices
  • Peer activities (peer coaching)

Challenging students

Unmotivated student

Look at the reason why the student is unmotivated, boredom, power struggle, level too hard or too easy?

Boredom:

  • Talk to the student to get to know his or her interests
  • Use technology, humor, body movement, story to teach
  • Less floor time, break it up, keep it challenging and fun
  • Use technology (audio record, then transcribe)

Power struggle:

  • Goal setting, set realistic goal to make the student to track own progress
  • Create choices

Level too easy or too hard:

  • Check assessment data, make sure lesson content targets the ZPD
  • Differentiate tasks
  • Exit tickets

Too shy or afraid:

  • Create an academically safe environment where students can ask questions
  • Teach them how to ask questions and clarify misunderstandings
  • Create a classroom culture with growth mindset so mistakes are valued, e.g. My favorite mistake today.

Challenging student

  • Clear understanding of behavioral expectations
  • Reward chart
  • Behavior contract
