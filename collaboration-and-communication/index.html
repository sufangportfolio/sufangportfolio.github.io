With teachers

  • Regular verbal and e-mail communication
  • Team meetings and planning
  • Being actively involved in staff, Triad and PLT meetings
  • Communicating effectively with staff in my level about activities such as activity nights, sleepover, weekly activities and excursions
  • Attending professional learning sessions in order to report back to fellow staff
  • Communicating effectively with educational support/members from SSG of students with special needs
  • Communicating with Literacy and Numeracy coaches for guidance and feedback
  • Using Google Drive to collaborate and communicate with the whole staff online
  • Informing fellow colleagues about any well-being issues that arise
  • Taking opportunities to observe how other teachers teach
  • Seeking feedback on lesson ideas or potential improvements to activities

With students

  • Creating clear routine and verbal/non-verbal signals
  • Prioritising getting to know our students by talking to them about their interests and lives outside of school
  • Setting clear and high expectations for student behaviour and achievement
  • Using a combination of visual and verbal communication
  • Providing a safe classroom environment through social circles, student letters and restorative practice
  • Encouraging risk taking to achieve
